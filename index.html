About Me

Who I am

I am a 23 year-old software developer from Atlanta. I grew up in Marietta, GA and attended the University of Georgia where I majored in Biology and graduated in spring 2019 with a B.S. in Biology. The semester after graduation I decided to pursue coding because it was something I always had an interest in and I wanted to work in an industry that I enjoyed. I had taught myself basic html, css and javascript, however I wanted a more formal education so I decided to enroll in the Georgia Tech coding boot camp after my final semester in Athens. I began my coding boot camp in Febuary 2020 and finished in August 2020. Aside from coding, I enjoy being active and playing/watching sports. I'm a huge college football and soccer fan, but I do watch all of the Atlanta sports teams regularly. I enjoy the outdoors and I am hoping to travel more once I get settled down and begin my career in software development.

My Accomplishments

I graduated high school in May 2015 with Honors. I had a 3.9 GPA and 9 AP classes to my name. I then attended the University of Georgia the ensuing fall semester. After 4 1/2 years at UGA, I graduated with honors with a B.S. in Biology while also having been directly involved in over 7 clubs and organizations on campus. After graduating UGA, I pursued Georgia Tech coding boot camp and graduated in August 2020. During boot camp, I completed over 20 individual assignments and projects, while also completing 3 team projects where I had to interact with my team on a daily basis. What I learned throughout my schooling was that time management and the ability to quickly learn new information was and continues to be vital to my success. I always adapted to situations I was presented and I always found a way to get my job done while also helping others when they needed. I learned that being able to communicate with my peers and my professors was essential for my long-term success. I believe that my ability to quickly adapt to new situation as well as my ability to work well alongside others makes me a good candidate for development.

My Future Goals

The picture above was taken during my time in Cuzco, Peru in May of 2018. I, alongside 14 others from various colleges around the southeast, traveled to Cuzco for two weeks to embark on a medical trip. My team, which consisted pre-dental students, traveled alongside a pre-med team. Together we partered with local doctors and dentists to set up a free heathcare clinic for underserved communities outside of Cuzco. We actively helped our respective medical leaders attend to patients for close to 10 hours a day 10 of the 14 days we were in Peru. This was by far the most rewarding experience of my life. My previous goal before switching to software development was to join Doctors Without Borders and to devote a portion of my life to a humanitarian cause. My current goal is to help great organizations like Doctors Without Borders in a new role providing infrastructure and tech to humanitarian organizations.
